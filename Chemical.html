Contraption

Description

Rules

Co-ordinators

Participate Now

Participation fees: 50 ₹ per person

Participate Now

1. Maximum 5 members per team are allowed.
2. Maximum 5 minutes will be provided to complete one run.
3. Points will be calculated based on
Total no. of steps involved. (2 Points per step)
Total no. of energy conversions. (4 Points per step)
Successful completion of final step. (10 Points per step)
4. If team members believe that touch is required to maintain continuity of their run, they have to inform to the coordinators first before implementing it. Rules for physical touch are:
Maximum 2 physical touches will be allowed by each team. (No negative marking)
After 2 touches, 3 additional touches will be allowed. (Negative marking) i.e. 1st & 2nd attempt – no negative; 3rd attempt: -1; 4th attempt: -2; 5th attempt: -3.
Completion of run without touch will be eligible for bonus point (+2 points).
Team having more than total 5 touches in one run will be disqualified.
5. You have to include minimum 3 energy conversions in your contraption model.
6. Consecutive energy conversions can’t be consider as energy conversion (i.e. transformation from mechanical energy to mechanical energy can’t be consider as energy conversion)
7. If same objects have been consequently placed, it will be considered as 1 step only.
8. Submit following details in soft copy before at least 1 day of the event.
Block diagram of your contraption setup.
Total no. of steps proposed by team
Total no. of energy conversions proposed by team
Approximate Area required for the setup
9. All the participants must be present before 2 hrs of the event timing. During that 2 hrs each team will be provided specified area to arrange their respective setup. All the participants must complete their contraption setup within 2 hrs. No additionally time will be provided to arrange the setup.

Note: 2nd run will be provided to each team (2nd run will be provided based on number of total entries. Decision will be taken by faculty coordinator only). If 2nd run has been provided to each team, best performed run will be considered.
If two teams are having same points, the team will be considered as a winner which will have maximum no. of energy conversions.
It is compulsory for participants to bring college I-card at the time of event.
The organizers have the right to change or update the rules at any time and will do best to inform.
Note: Judge Decision will be final.

Chem-O-Cryst

Description

Rules

Co-ordinators

Participate Now

A known amount of solid material (Chemical) will be given to the participants. Four to five different solvents will be provided. The solid material will be solubilised in a one of the given solvents. The cooling mechanism must be used for nucleation growth. Crystals' quality and quantity will be analysed after filtration and drying.
Objective
To crystallize the given solid material with proper solvent.

Participate Now

Students from any discipline can participate in the event.
Team Size: maximum 2 participants per team.
Registration fees: 50 Rs. per Person.
For Crystal growth, participant must follow the path of cooling.
Cooling time must not exceed 10 minutes and total experiment time must not exceed 60 minutes. Extra time usage will lead to minus marks (-1 marks for every 5 minutes extra in dissolution and cooling mechanism).
Further procedure and instructions related to the experiment will be given at a time of event.

Winning Criteria:
1. Selection of Solvent (Preferably with Justification)
2. Crystal Quantity and Quality (Size and Shape).
3. Additional marks.
4. Selection of Solvent and Justification : 2 points
5. Optimum amount of solvent used : 1 point
6. Use of inventories : 1 point
7. Solute Dissolution time (20 minutes: for every extra 5 min.): -1 Point
  8. Cooling time (10 Minutes: for every extra 5 min.) : -1 Point

Note:
• It is compulsory for participants to bring college I-card and apron along with them at the time of event.
• Required chemicals, glass wares and other requirements will be provided.
• The organizers have the right to change or update the rules at any time and will do best to inform.
• The decision of co-ordinators/Judges will be final.
